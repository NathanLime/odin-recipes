A crispy, savory, and spicy Korean pancake made with chopped fermented kimchi.

Prep Time: 10 mins | Cook Time: 15 mins | Serves: 2-3

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Well-Fermented Kimchi (chopped into small pieces)
  • 1/3 cup Kimchi Brine (the juice from the kimchi)
  • 1 cup All-Purpose Flour (or Korean pancake mix)
  • 1/4 cup Potato Starch or Cornstarch (for crispiness)
  • 1 cup Cold Water (or club soda for extra crispiness)
  • 1/2 Onion (thinly sliced)
  • 2 Green Onions (sliced)
  • 1 tsp Sugar (to balance sourness)
  • 1 tsp Gochugaru (optional, for extra color/spice)
  • Neutral Oil (Canola, Vegetable, or Rice Bran oil, for frying)

Optional Dipping Sauce

  • 2 tbsp Soy Sauce, 1 tbsp Vinegar, 1 tsp Sugar, 1 tsp Water

Instructions

  1. Mix Dry Batter: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, potato starch, sugar, and Gochugaru (if using).
  2. Add Wet Ingredients: Add the cold water and kimchi brine to the dry mixture. Mix gently until just combined (some lumps are fine; do not overmix, as this develops gluten and makes the pancake chewy, not crispy).
  3. Fold in Fillings: Fold in the chopped kimchi, sliced onion, and sliced green onions. The batter should be slightly thick but still pourable, with the kimchi and vegetables evenly coated.
  4. Fry the Pancake: Heat a non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Add a generous amount of oil (about 2-3 tablespoons) and swirl to coat the pan. Pour about 1/3 of the batter into the hot oil and spread it into a thin, even circle.
  5. Cook Until Crispy: Cook for 3-4 minutes until the bottom is golden brown and crispy, and the edges pull away easily. Add a little more oil around the edges, then flip. Press down gently with a spatula to ensure even crispiness. Cook the second side for another 3-4 minutes.
  6. Slice and Serve: Transfer the pancake to a cutting board, slice into wedges, and serve immediately with the dipping sauce on the side.
