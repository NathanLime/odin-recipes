Doenjang Jjigae (Korean Soybean Paste Stew)

Description

A classic, earthy Korean stew featuring savory fermented soybean paste and fresh vegetables.

Prep Time: 15 mins | Cook Time: 25 mins | Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 3 cups Stock (Anchovy-Kelp stock, Rice Water, or Vegetable Stock)
  • 2-3 tbsp Doenjang (Korean fermented soybean paste)
  • 1/2 cup Zucchini (cut into half-moons)
  • 1/2 cup Potato (cut into thick slices)
  • 1/2 Onion (cut into chunks)
  • 1 block Firm Tofu (cubed)
  • 2 cloves Garlic (minced)
  • 1 tsp Gochugaru (Korean chili flakes - optional)
  • 1 tsp Gochujang (Korean chili paste - optional, for color/spice)
  • 1 Green Chili/Cheongyang Pepper (sliced - optional)
  • 2 Green Onions/Scallions (sliced for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Prepare Broth and Paste: If using anchovy/kelp, simmer them in water to make the stock and strain. In a small bowl, whisk the Doenjang and Gochujang (if using) into 1/2 cup of the prepared stock to help it dissolve smoothly.
  2. Build the Stew Base: Add the remaining stock to a pot and bring to a boil. Add the potato slices and simmer for 5-7 minutes as they take the longest to cook.
  3. Add Flavor and Vegetables: Add the dissolved Doenjang mixture, minced garlic, and Gochugaru (if using) to the pot. Stir well. Add the zucchini and onion chunks and continue to boil for 5-8 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Finish and Serve: Gently add the cubed tofu and sliced chili pepper (if using). Simmer for 2-3 minutes until the tofu is heated through. Taste and add a pinch of salt or more Doenjang if needed. Garnish generously with sliced green onions and serve hot with steamed rice.
